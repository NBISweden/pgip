Reference genome

Once individual re-sequencing data has been obtained, it is mapped to a reference genome. For non-model organisms, reference genomes are often lacking1, for which reason many studies include sequencing and genome assembly of a reference individual. In other cases, a solution is to map to a closely related species. That comes with issues of its own, such as reads primarily mapping to conserved regions, thereby biasing population genomic statistics, or structural rearrangements between species hampering inferences. A final option is to map reads to a reference transcriptome. However, given the drop in sequencing cost and advances in long-read technologies, new projects could be complemented with de novo assembly of a reference individual.
